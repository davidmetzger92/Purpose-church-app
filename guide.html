Editing the App

How to update the photos, buttons, and text on the Purpose app.

Takes about a minute
First time only · ~5 min

Get set up

You only do these two things once.

1
Make a free GitHub account at github.com/signup.
You can use your normal email. Save your username and password somewhere safe.
2
Accept the invite. David will email you an invite to the project — open it and click Accept.
This is what lets your changes save. Without it, edits won't stick.
Every time

Make a change

Open the editor, change what you need, hit Save.

1
Open the editor and click Sign In with GitHub.
Keep the editor link to yourself — it's the staff door, not the public app.
2
Click App Content → Home Screen.
3
Change what you need (see below), then click Save in the top-right.
4
Wait about a minute, then open or refresh the app to see it.
Already-open apps update on the next refresh.
Open the Editor
The fields

What you can edit

Everything on the app's home screen, in one form.

Hero

The big photo at the top — headline (e.g. "Take Your Next Step"), service times, and the background photo.

Buttons (photo bands)

The stacked photo links. Add, remove, reorder, or rename them. Each has a label, an icon (dropdown), a link, and its own background photo.

Discover footer

The bottom photo band — heading, a short blurb, and the background photo.

Photos & cropping

Make photos fit

The bands are wide and short, so photos get cropped. Two easy ways to control it.

Best: use the Crop Helper below — drag a box over your photo and download a version that fits perfectly, then upload that.
Quick: after uploading, use the Image Focus dropdown (Top / Center / Bottom) to slide which part of the photo shows.
Open the Crop Helper
Good to know

You can't break it

A few things that make this stress-free.

You're only editing content — the design and layout are locked. Worst case is a typo; just fix it and Save again.
To add a button, use + Add under Buttons. Icons come from a dropdown, so you can't mistype them.
Stuck or unsure? Don't force it — text David before changing anything you're not confident about.
